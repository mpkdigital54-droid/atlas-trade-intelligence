Financial Instruments

Every Major Instrument
We Facilitate

From Letters of Credit to blockchain settlements, our expertise spans the full spectrum of trade finance instruments.

Select an instrument category to explore our capabilities in that area.

Sight LC
Payment made immediately upon presentation of compliant documents. Ideal for one-off high-value transactions.
Immediate
Usance LC
Payment after a specified period (30, 60, 90 days) from bill of lading or sight. Provides buyer with liquidity buffer.
Deferred
Confirmed LC
A second bank adds its confirmation, guaranteeing payment regardless of issuing bank risk.
Guaranteed
Standby LC (SBLC)
Performance guarantee drawn only if the applicant fails to fulfill an obligation. Governs ISP98.
Guarantee
Revolving LC
Covers multiple shipments under a single LC, automatically replenishing after each draw.
Recurring
Transferable LC
Allows the beneficiary to transfer credit to a second beneficiary. Common in commodity trading chains.
Transferable
Back-to-Back LC
Two separate LCs for ultimate buyer and supplier, used when an intermediary is involved in the chain.
Intermediary
Governed by UCP 600 — Uniform Customs and Practice for Documentary Credits
Performance Bond
Guarantees the seller's performance under the contract terms and delivery conditions.
Performance
Advance Payment Guarantee
Secures any advance payment made by the buyer, ensuring return if seller defaults.
Advance
Bid / Tender Bond
Ensures the bidder will honor the contract if awarded the bid. Common in large procurement.
Tender
Payment Guarantee
Guarantees the buyer's payment obligation to the seller under the trade contract.
Payment
Documents Against Payment (D/P)
Buyer pays at sight to obtain shipping documents. Lower cost than LC, moderate protection.
D/P
Documents Against Acceptance (D/A)
Buyer accepts a time draft (promise to pay later) to obtain documents. URC 522 governed.
D/A
Open Account
Seller ships and invoices; buyer pays at an agreed future date (Net 30/60/90). Common in established relationships.
Open
Cash in Advance
Full or partial payment before shipment. Minimizes seller risk, burdens buyer with upfront capital.
Advance
Governed by URC 522 — Uniform Rules for Collections
Bills of Exchange
Sight drafts payable on demand; time drafts payable at a fixed future date. Used under LCs or collections.
Draft
Promissory Notes
Written promise by the buyer to pay a specified amount to the seller on a certain date.
Promise
Bankers' Acceptances
A time draft accepted by a bank — a negotiable instrument tradeable in the money market.
Negotiable
SWIFT MT103
Single customer credit transfer. The standard message type for cross-border international payments.
SWIFT
SWIFT MT202
Bank-to-bank cover payment. Used for interbank settlements and cover payments (MT202 COV).
SWIFT
SWIFT MT760
Issuance of Standby LCs and Bank Guarantees via the global SWIFT messaging network.
SWIFT
Forfaiting
Seller sells medium-term receivables at a discount to a forfaiter on a without-recourse basis.
Without Recourse
Factoring
Seller assigns accounts receivable to a factor for immediate cash, typically with recourse.
Receivables
Trade Credit Insurance
Insures the seller against buyer default, enabling open account terms with protection.
Insurance
Supply Chain Finance
Bank pays seller early at discount based on buyer's creditworthiness. Reverse factoring model.
SCF
Payment Netting
Offsetting receivables and payables between parties. Reduces gross settlement risk and cost.
Netting
Multi-Currency Pools
Consolidation and reallocation of bulk cross-border treasury funds across multiple currencies.
Treasury
Digital Letters of Credit
Electronic LCs via platforms like Bolero, essDocs, and TradeLens on distributed ledger technology.
DLT
Crypto & Digital Assets
Secure, large-volume conversion and transfer of digital assets to fiat currency for institutional settlement.
Crypto
Tokenized Payments
Blockchain-based tokenized payment settlement for cross-border high-value transactions.
Tokenized
SWIFT GPI Tracking
Global Payments Innovation — enhanced tracking and transparency for all cross-border payments.
GPI
